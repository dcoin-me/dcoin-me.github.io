New Type of Arbitration

Online arbitration or how to clear all doubts regarding your credibility. When shopping online, there is always a risk to run into an unfair seller or buyer. And it is often that you’re the only one the problem resolution depends on. Even dealing with well-known online shopping platforms you can still have issues with the person making a decision regarding your dispute. That's how the idea of arbitration appeared.

Basic Principles:

  • Anyone can become the arbitrator. Every arbitrator has own statistics of the decisions. Arbitrators get fee from every deal in which they were chosen as arbitrators.
  • To make a deal both seller and buyer must have the same arbitrator (at least one arbitrator in common).
  • seller can specify number of days during which buyer can ask for money back (refund). Also seller can specify percentage of the purchase price, which is held at the seller's expense for the duration specified before.

Here is an example of the deal:

First of all, buyer have to choose trusted arbitrators


Every arbitrator’s actions are recorded and saved in the blockchain. Thus, information about the arbitrator is public. But for wrapping protection, such statistics is only available to known users.


User chooses an arbitrator from the list of trusted arbiters (both by seller and buyer) and clicks “send”.


If buyer has any claims against the seller, he/she goes to the list of purchases and clicks “moneyback”, and specifies his/her email. E-mail is encrypted with a public (open) key of the arbitrator and the seller. Buyer has to wait for an answer from the arbitrator.

Now Details

Foreword about DCoin

Dcoin has no central servers. As in BitCoin, every node has a full data base copy. New data exchange between nodes takes place via blocks. Every block has a hash of the previous hash. All transactions from one user have unique signature, which proves that such transaction was made by a particular user.


In contrast to BitCoin (where there is only one type of transaction), Dcoin has 66 transactions types.

Mining in Dcoin

Mining with Dcoin is not the mining everyone is used to dealing with in context of other cryptocurrencies. It is effortless. Dcoins are created with every new block and shared among all participants. For anonymous users, coin volume growth is approx. 0.0004% per block. % value is determined by voting of identified users.

One identified user can create a limited number of coins from nothing. In other words, users, who proved their authenticity, are responsible for creating primary amount of coins. Every such user is also an offline exchange “point” to change cash for coins. Identified users are called MINERS

If the amount of coins becomes too high for market demands, coins reduction happens. Reduction is based on the data from the blockchain, and so it can be forecasted, relying upon the number of miners, coins and promised amounts. As the number of miners increases, reduction becomes unfeasible.

Details on Arbitration

Multy-arbitration

DCoin gives an option to involve up to 5 arbitrators per one deal. In case of “moneyback” request, any of 5 trusted arbitrators can deal with it. After the transaction is entered to the blockchain, the list of involved arbitrators can’t be changed. Every arbotrator involved in the transaction receives a commission.

List of Trusted Arbitratiors

To make a deal both seller and buyer must have the same arbitrator.

Holdback

Seller can specify number of days for money back request. He can also specify the % of holdback.

For pizza-delivery company it can be 3 days and 10% holdback.

For the new seller using ex/.Alibaba.com, it would be better to specify 30 days and 100% holdback. If reputation increases, holdback% can be reduced. 100% holdback means holding the entire amount the buyer has paid for 30 days and it can be refunded if an arbitrator makes a decision for moneyback.

Satisfying Moneyback Request

Moneyback request is visible for seller and arbitrator. If seller was able to reach an agreement with buyer before the arbitrator’s decision, Moneyback can be refunded by seller. If seller didn’t make full refund, arbitrator can do this. Moneyback can’t exceed the sum of transaction.

Drawbacks

The main drawback is that an arbitrator has to be a highly trusted person among sellers/buyers and have a reputation of a fair judge, not wasting time on full investigations of every dispute.

Resume

As a result, benefits of independence ensured by decentralization of e-money are supplemented with one of the major advantages of traditional payment instruments: possibility to dispute a translation.
